French Toast

Description

French toast is a dish made of sliced bread soaked in beaten eggs and typically milk, then pan fried. Alternative names and variants include "eggy bread",[1] "Bombay toast", "gypsy toast",[2] and "poor knights" (of Windsor).[3] When French toast is served as a sweet dish, milk, sugar, vanilla or cinnamon are also commonly added before pan-frying, and then it may be topped with sugar (often powdered sugar), butter, fruit, or syrup. When it is a savory dish, it is generally fried with a pinch of salt or pepper, and it can then be served with a sauce such as ketchup or mayonnaise.[4][5][6][7]

Ingredients

  • 4 slices bread
  • 1 egg (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon powder
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 1-2 cups milk

Steps

  1. Cut the bread slices diagonally in half.
  2. In a deep bowl, pour in milk
  3. Crack the egg into the milk and beat the mixture until the egg had been fully incorporated. Skip this step if you are not using eggs.
  4. If you use sugar, you may already dissolve it into the liquid ingredients.
  5. Add oil and butter into the frying panand heat it until very hot. Oil and butter will prevent the butter from burning too soon and enhance the flavor.
  6. Very carefully, place 4 slices of bread into the hot oil.
  7. Sprinkle cinnamon and serve.
